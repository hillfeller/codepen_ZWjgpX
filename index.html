About Me

I am a budding web developer with solid Ruby on Rails foundations, as well as that of front end technologies. I am increasinly interested in both front and backend web development. I'm on the lookout for new technologies, always troubleshooting and always learning. At my most recent position I was employed at a software company called AvePoint as a senior technical analyst, as well as support manager. While troubleshooting the proprietary application, entitled DocaAve used for content management, and observing its complex relationship with SharePoint, I worked daily with both the development team as well as project managers to interpret the logs, on the lookout for bugs and unusual patterns of software behavior.

It soon became clear to me that knowing the company's software wasn't enough - I would need to know and understand its underlying code. Subsequently, I enrolled in an apprenticeship with Bloc which provided excellent RoR foundations. This included CRUD actions, model-view-controller architecture, including but not limited to authenticating and authorizing users, signing in, and creating public profiles. Additionally, I have focused on utilizing RSpec with regards to utilizing TDD.

Whether the Weather

Whether the Weather serves as a user community for individuals that experience joint point during inclement weather.

In addition to providing weather updates for specific locations, users may also confirm local weather, as well as follow other users. Within the user profile a graph is generated based on the number of posts that they have assigned a weather label.

As joint ailments play a central role to the application, users can update their ailments.
